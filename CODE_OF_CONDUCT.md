# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Contributors and users must communicate professionally, review evidence fairly and protect the privacy and security of people and organizations affected by audit work.

Expected behavior includes:

- respectful, constructive technical discussion;
- clear separation of evidence, inference and opinion;
- responsible handling of vulnerabilities and sensitive data;
- acknowledgment of limitations and uncertainty;
- proportionate recommendations that avoid unnecessary complexity;
- respect for different experience levels, backgrounds and accessibility needs.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, discrimination or personal attacks;
- public disclosure of live credentials, exploit details or private data;
- pressuring contributors to weaken safety or release controls;
- using the project to facilitate unauthorized testing or harmful activity;
- misrepresenting limited audit results as certification or proof of zero risk;
- knowingly fabricating test results or evidence.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit, remove or reject contributions and interactions that violate this standard. Serious security or privacy issues should be handled through `SECURITY.md`, not public discussion.

## Scope

This code applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, examples and other project spaces.
